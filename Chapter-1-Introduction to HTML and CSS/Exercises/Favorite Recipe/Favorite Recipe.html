Bistek Tagalog (Filipino Beef Steak)

Bistek, adapted from the phrase “beef steak” and the Spanish beef dish “bistec,”
and similar to the Italian word bistecca, is one of the more popular ways Filipinos cook beef.
The thin, tender slices of beef are braised in citrus and soy sauce until tender.

Bistek is such a crowd-pleaser that it can transcend from an everyday family meal to elegant party fare.
Serve this quick and easy savory dish with a side of steamed rice and a veggie for a wholesome meal.

Main Ingredients

  • 1⁄2 Sliced of Pounds Beef Sirloin or Skirt Steak
  • 11⁄2 Teaspoons Baking Soda
  • 1⁄2 Cup Calamansi Juice, Fresh or Frozen Concentrate (or Lemon Juice)
  • 1⁄4 Cup Soy Sauce (like Silver Swan brand)
  • 1⁄2 Teaspoon freshly ground Black Pepper
  • 2 Tablespoons Vegetable Oil
  • 1 Medium (6 to 10 ounce) White or Yellow Onion, sliced in rings
  • 2 Cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1⁄4 Cup Beef Broth or Water
  • Salt of your preference, as needed
  • 4 Cups Steamed Rice, for serving

Cooking Instructions

STEP 1: Prepare the Beef

Pound each slice of beef with a mallet to tenderize. Place in a bowl and set aside.



STEP 2: Marinate the Beef

In a bowl, Add the calamansi juice (or lemon), soy sauce, and black pepper to the beef.
Incorporate the ingredients well. Marinate the beef for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
If your kitchen is warm, transfer the bowl to the fridge.



STEP 3: Sear the Beef

Remove the beef from the marinade including the garlic bits. Heat oil in a pan over medium-high heat.
When it starts to smoke, add the beef. Sear each side until brown crust forms.
Do this in batches. Do not overcrowd the pan. Transfer to a plate and set aside.



STEP 4: Cook the Bistek

Stir-fry half of the red and yellow onions. Add the marinade and water. Let it simmer for 2 minutes.
Add back beef including all the juices. Toss to coat the beef with the sauce. Do a taste test.
Add more calamansi and soy sauce if preferred. Season with salt and black pepper.



STEP 5: Plate & Serve

Arrange the beef on a large, rimmed serving platter. Pour the sauce over the beef.
Garnish the top of the meat with the reserved onions. Serve warm with steamed rice and enjoy!

You can keep the leftover bistek refrigerated in non-reactive food containers for up to 1 week.
You can also freeze for up to 1 month.
